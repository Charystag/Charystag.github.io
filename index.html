About Me

I'm a software engineer with a strong foundation in mathematics and programming, fueled by a lifelong passion for problem-solving and innovation.

My academic journey began at the Université Paris-Dauphine, where I discovered my fascination with probability, statistics, and numerical analysis, eventually specializing in artificial intelligence. Wanting to broaden my perspective beyond mathematics, I earned a degree in Philosophy from Paris-Sorbonne in 2022, deepening my critical thinking skills and appreciation for interdisciplinary approaches.

Alongside my studies, I honed my technical expertise at École 42, where I built a robust programming foundation. This experience helped me solidify my skills in languages like C, Python, C++, and Rust, and fueled my interest in automation. I’m passionate about streamlining workflows, leveraging tools like Bash scripting, and building custom solutions that make life—and work—more efficient.

Outside of coding, I'm deeply involved in the art of eloquence and public speaking. As the Vice-President of my university's debate team, I led my team to the quarter-finals, and I later took on the role of Vice-President of the French Federation of Debate and Eloquence. I also enjoy sharing my knowledge as a tech trainer, specializing in AI, data management, and foundational programming skills. Through both my professional and personal projects, I prioritize clear and concise documentation, knowing it’s essential for sustainable, bug-free development. I invite you to explore my repositories, where you’ll find personal projects focused on automation and a curated collection of learning resources aimed at enhancing programming skills.
